Weekly Report

In software development weekly update meetings are a common occurrence.  Sometimes called "standup" or "Scrum" meetings they are intended to be brief but complete accountability reports of each team members work since the last meeting. Awareness to problems can be raised...but this is not the place to solve them.

A standup will generally involve each person taking about a minute to answer 3 questions:

  1. What did I do since the last meeting?
  2. What am I going to do next?
  3. What are my blockers?

Blockers are anything that is keeping you from accomplishing your goals. They can include troublesome bugs, broken code, design questions that you just can't come up with a solution for, they can even be other people. "I just didn't get around to it..." is not a blocker...it is however a good way to get removed from projects and ultimately fired ;)

Each week you will be required to complete a report addressing the three questions above.  Please complete this thoughtfully, and be specific.  For the second question (What am I going to do this week?) don't just say "Continue working on my project". Instead explain what you hope to get done on your project...ie "I will implement the add, subtract, and multiply functionality, including adding buttons to control when the functions are called".

Ideally one could go and look at the code repository for your project and see commits that match up with what you report at the end of the week!Don't skip over the blockers question! This will be your best path to let the instructor know how they can best help you.
