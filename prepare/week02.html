Preparation

This week all of the assigned readings will be from the JavaScript: Novice to Ninja (Sitepoint) book. Remember though, that if you are having trouble understanding a concept the way that this book presents it, go find another source!

Keep the following Tips from Week 01 in mind as well.

Tips

  • Read for understanding, not completion. This means that some weeks when the concepts settle quickly in your mind you will fly through the reading. Week 01 is a good example. It's review and most of you should get through it fairly quickly. However, in other weeks you might have to read through the text several times and spend a lot of time working through the code examples until you understand. You may even need to go find a few extra sources on your own for some topics.
  • Dedicate sufficient time to your studies! For many weeks, the reading will make up most of the assigned work for the course. This means that most weeks you ought to plan on 5–6 hours for the reading assignments. Some of you will spend most of the time reading and understanding the concepts. Others you may spend most of the time working on the exercises. Play to your strengths and spend your time where it will benefit you and your learning style the most.
  • Don't skip the code examples! Make sure to spend time reviewing these. The book you are using allows you to interact with the code samples directly. If you have time you should also work through the exercises at the end of each chapter.
  • Take notes. One of the most important things that you can include in your notes are questions that arose as you were reading. Having this list will help you to know what topics need more time, and will give you something to say when the instructor asks: "Any questions from the reading?"
  • If chapters from the book weren't assigned, it is because you should already understand those concepts from a previous course. However, you may want to read some of the unassigned chapters if the topics look unfamiliar to you.

  • As a starting point, here are some additional resources you have free access to. Again, don't be afraid to find your own sources, sometimes the way an author explains a concept will not compute with your brain. Seek out another explanation!

Reading

  1. Read Chapter 2: Programming Basics. Much of this should be review, but pay special attention to the sections on variables, scope, converting/parsing, and template literals.
  2. Read all of Chapter 3: Arrays, Logic, and Loops. Again much of this should feel like review. Pay special attention to the section on array methods.
  3. Read all of Chapter 4: Functions. Note that there are multiple ways to declare a function in Javascript. Pay close attention to the concept of callbacks as well.

  4. If you are looking for more code exercises to practice with check out this Code Sandbox. You should look at the exercises from Chapter 2–4

  5. Alternatively (or additionally) you could looks at chapters 2–4 of Eloquent JavaScript (EJS) if you feel like you need a different approach or more depth on these topics.
