Preparation

As you begin with the first preparation activity for the course; it is crucial that you understand the importance of preparation. Do not treat these activities as a task to check off in as little time as possible. Instead, always treat them as opportunities to learn.

It is assumed that you know and understand the concepts from the Prepare activities. The other activities and discussions will be designed to move beyond the readings: these activities give examples and use cases, and talk about potential problems. It is extremely important that you ask for clarification on parts of the readings that didn't make sense. Your instructor is happy to discuss them with you.

Tips

  • Read for understanding, not completion. This means that some weeks when the concepts settle quickly in your mind you will fly through the reading. Week 01 is a good example. It's review and most of you should get through it fairly quickly. However, in other weeks you might have to read through the text several times and spend a lot of time working through the code examples until you understand. You may even need to go find a few extra sources on your own for some topics.
  • Dedicate sufficient time to your studies! For many weeks, the reading will make up most of the assigned work for the course. This means that most weeks you ought to plan on 5â€“6 hours for the reading assignments. Some of you will spend most of the time reading and understanding the concepts. Others you may spend most of the time working on the exercises. Play to your strengths and spend your time where it will benefit you and your learning style the most.
  • Don't skip the code examples! Make sure to spend time reviewing these. The book you are using allows you to interact with the code samples directly. If you have time you should also work through the exercises at the end of each chapter.
  • Take notes. One of the most important things that you can include in your notes are questions that arose as you were reading. Having this list will help you to know what topics need more time, and will give you something to say when the instructor asks: "Any questions from the reading?"
  • If chapters from the book weren't assigned, it is because you should already understand those concepts from a previous course. However, you may want to read some of the unassigned chapters if the topics look unfamiliar to you.

  • Don't be afraid to find your own sources. Sometimes the way an author explains a concept will not compute with your brain. If that's the case, find another explanation! As a starting point, here are some resources you have free access to.

    • Javascript: Novice to Ninja (Sitepoint)
    • Eloquent JavaScript (EJS)
    • You don't know JS
    • Exploring ES6
    • MDN web docs

Reading

  1. Read the Syllabus

  2. Then read through all of Doing Stuff with Web Things. Most of this should be review, but there will be a few new ideas in there for most students. Pay special attention to the parts on Local Storage.

  3. Watch the video Master Mobile UX (32min) on Sitepoint. Be prepared to discuss what you have learned with your classmates.
  4. In I-Learn, turn in your notes (including questions) or turn in the code from the code examples and exercises.
